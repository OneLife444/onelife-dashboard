Strategic Notes & Warnings

Read before applying
🚫 ScotiaRISE — Remove All 12-Step Language
Scotiabank explicitly excludes 12-step programs from ScotiaRISE funding. Any application that mentions AA, NA, 12-step, or step-based programming will be disqualified. Frame One Life's work entirely around housing stability, employment outcomes, and psychosocial support.
⚠️ FNHA Harm Reduction — Needs Indigenous Co-Applicant
One Life cannot apply for FNHA Harm Reduction Grants directly — eligibility requires an Indigenous organization as lead or co-applicant. Build the Tillicum Lelum or Snuneymuxw partnership first, then apply jointly. The $50K grant becomes accessible only through that relationship.
⚠️ TELUS — Confirm Resident Age Profile First
TELUS Friendly Future Foundation requires programs to focus on youth up to age 29. Before applying, Brett needs to confirm what percentage of current and recent residents are under 30. If it's a minority, this application is a stretch and may not be worth the effort.
💡 Vancouver Foundation — Framing Is Everything
The Transforming Systems Grant (up to $300K over 3 years) requires One Life to position itself as transforming Nanaimo's recovery housing system — not just operating beds. The narrative must address root causes of inequality and describe how One Life's model could scale or influence sector practice. Engage a grant writer for this one.
💡 Lululemon — Budget Check Required
Grant cannot exceed 40% of One Life's most recent fiscal year operating budget. If annual revenue is ~$216K (6 beds × $3K/month), cap is ~$86K — well above the grant max, so likely fine. But confirm exact revenue with Brett's financials before July application window.
💡 Construction Industry — Pitch as Pipeline, Not Charity
IWC Excavation, Knappett Industries, and VICA's 450+ member companies need reliable workers. One Life produces employment-ready men in recovery. This pitch is more sustainable than a grant request because it frames One Life as a workforce partner solving a business problem. Brett needs to lead this conversation in person.
